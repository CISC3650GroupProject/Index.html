Below is some info about the art collection:

Welcome to the online catalogue of Brooklyn College Library's art collection, which includes paintings, drawings,
sculptures, photography, and prints.



Diverse in character and international in scope, the collection features art by Elizabeth Murray, William Kentridge,
Chakaia Booker, Shahzia Sikander, Sarah Sze, John Walker, Edward Ruscha, and Xu Bing. Several of these artists live and
work in Brooklyn's dynamic art community, and they are all internationally recognized, with works in major museums around
the world.

Most of the art collection was acquired just before the 2002 opening of the newly renovated and expanded Library.
Many of the purchases were funded by New York City's "Percent for Art" law, which ensures that public spaces are graced
with art. Numerous other works were acquired through gifts, and some works are on loan. One very special work, Olindo
Mario Ricci's Famous Libraries of the World mural, was funded in 1936 by the Federal Arts Project.
